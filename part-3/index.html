Part 3: Quick start

A seven-phase, end-to-end walkthrough from a sealed box to live inventory and back. Phase 0 covers prerequisites; Phase 2 splits into Direct (123RFID Desktop) and Bridged (123RFID Mobile) variants.

📄️Your first 30 minutes

A 30-minute IOTC walkthrough — from sealed box to live tag reads in seven phases. Phase 0 prerequisites; Phase 2 splits Direct (Desktop) vs Bridged (Mobile).

🗃Phase 0: Prerequisites

3 items

📄️Phase 1: Prepare network and broker

Phase 1 of the IOTC Quick Start: prepare the network and broker. By the end you have a reachable MQTT 3.1.1 broker and credentials.

🗃Phase 2: Bootstrap (Direct or Bridged)

2 items

📄️Phase 3: Verify the bootstrap connection

Phase 3 of the IOTC Quick Start: send your first MQTT command (get_version) and read the response. Proves the reader is reachable and ready for Phase 4.

📄️Phase 4: Inspect endpoint state

Phase 4 of the IOTC Quick Start: send control_operation with operation=START to begin reading tags. Operating-mode prerequisites and response semantics.

📄️Phase 5: Add remote endpoints

Phase 5 of the IOTC Quick Start: subscribe to dataEVT and watch tag reads stream in. Covers the schema, channel selection, and live filtering.

📄️Phase 6: Start and stop inventory

Phase 6 of the IOTC Quick Start: cleanly stop inventory with control_operation STOP. When to use STOP vs trigger button vs disconnect to halt reads.

📄️Phase 7: Reboot when needed

Phase 7 of the IOTC Quick Start: when and how to reboot the reader. Covers what survives a reboot (most config) vs what is reset (active inventory state).
